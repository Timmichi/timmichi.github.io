About

My name is Timothy Simanhadi, and I'm a Junior at UC Irvine studying Computer Science. If you don't know who I am outside of my LinkedIn profile or resume, then you probably already know my skillsets and qualifications. However, this page will show you my journey; something an 8.5 x 11 inch pdf can't tell you.

There's plenty of smart people to go around. But people who can empathize with others or see their skills as a means for something greater than themselves? Unfortunately not much of that. For most of my life before college, I kept good grades but I really learned nothing. I went to church, but only because I was forced to. I was a cog in a machine called "life".

But by the end of 2020 I'll have:

  • ⁌ Competed in my first Hackathon ⁍
  • ⁌ Found my passion for programming ⁍
  • ⁌ Developed my first passion project ⁍
  • ⁌ Raised $2000 to fly to Japan and spread the gospel ⁍
  • ⁌ Built lasting relationships ⁍
  • ⁌ Became a board member for a club I care about ⁍
  • ⁌ Started undergraduate research in IOT and AI ⁍
  • ⁌ Canvassed to hundreds of people for Doctors without Borders ⁍
  • ⁌ Landed my first job in the field of tech ⁍
  • ⁌ Learned how to live on my own ⁍
  • ⁌ Started making my own decisions ⁍
  • ⁌ Learned how to overcome rejections ⁍
  • ⁌ Found my purpose in life ⁍

These are the things I want to convey that I can't fit on my resume. For me, this is much more than a check list. It's a manifestation of what I'm proud of and who I've come to be. Being raised by loving parents who worked hard to create an environment for me to succeed, I always felt a need to chase success. And sometimes I still do. But now I know success has a different meaning. All these blessings only motivate me to use my skills to help people who don't have the advantages and opportunities I have.
