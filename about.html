About

My name is Timothy Simanhadi, and I'm a Junior at UC Irvine studying Computer Science.

If you don't know me already, then one thing you should know is if I can find a way to help others, I will. And I'll make sure to bring it full circle. No loose ends. No half promises. Often, you have people who are smart, but they can't work in a team, empathize with others, or see their skills as a means for a greater goal (and no I don't mean money). For most of my life before college - minus being smart - I was like this. Just going through the motions - not knowing what I stood for, not having a passion for anything, and basically being a cog in the machine of life.

By the end of 2020 I will have:

  • ⁌ Competed in my first Hackathon ⁍
  • ⁌ Found my passion for programming ⁍
  • ⁌ Developed my first passion project ⁍
  • ⁌ Raised $2000 to fly to Japan and spread the gospel ⁍
  • ⁌ Solidified amazing friend groups ⁍
  • ⁌ Became a board member for a club I care about ⁍
  • ⁌ Started undergraduate research in IOT and AI ⁍
  • ⁌ Canvassed to hundreds of people for Doctors without Borders ⁍
  • ⁌ Landed my first job outside of the food industry ⁍
  • ⁌ Learned how to live on my own ⁍
  • ⁌ Started making my own decisions ⁍
  • ⁌ Faced rejection hundereds of times ⁍
  • ⁌ Started to understand my purpose ⁍

For me, these are big strides in personal growth. Being born and raised in Sacramento by loving parents who came from little and succeeded in achieving the American Dream, I always felt a need to prove something to them because I had such a privileged life. And sometimes I still do. But this is who I am, and these are the things I've done. Yes, I've had a ton of help. Yes, I have people who care about me. Yes, I am privileged. That's why I want to use what I've learned to help people who don't have the advantages and opportunities I have.

Thanks for coming to my ted talk. If you made it this far you a real one.
