About

My name is Timothy Simanhadi, and I'm a Junior at UC Irvine studying Computer Science. If you don't know who I am outside of my LinkedIn profile or resumé, then you probably already know my skillsets and qualifications. However, this page will show you my journey; something an 8.5 x 11 inch pdf can't tell you.

There's plenty of smart people to go around. But people who can truly empathize with others or see their skills as a means for something greater than themselves? Unfortunately not much of that. For most of my life before college, I kept good grades but I really retained nothing. I went to church, but only because I was forced to. I never stepped out of my bubble, because I was too afraid of failing.

But by the end of 2020 I'll have:

  • ⁌ Competed in my first Hackathon
  • ⁌Found my passion for programming
  • ⁌ Developed my first passion project
  • ⁌ Raised $2000 to fly to Japan and spread the gospel
  • ⁌ Built lasting relationships with friends
  • ⁌ Became a board member for a club I care about
  • ⁌ Started undergraduate research in IOT and AI
  • ⁌ Canvassed to hundreds of people for Doctors without Borders
  • ⁌ Landed my first tech gig
  • ⁌ Learned how to live on my own
  • ⁌ Found my purpose in life

These are the things I want to convey that I can't fit on my resumé. For me, this is much more than a check list. It's a manifestation of what I'm proud of and how I've grown these last 2 years. Being raised by loving parents who worked hard to create an environment for me to succeed, I always felt a need to chase success. And sometimes I still do. But now I know success has a different meaning. These blessings only motivate me to use my skills to help people who don't have the advantages and opportunities I have.
